Info

Programming languages have been part of my life since childhood. From the memories of MS-DOS, Visual Basic, Turbo Pascal and Norton Commander to the earliest versions of Windows 3.11, passed on by the young developers making use of a dozen floppy disks, pilled up on the computer tower fueled by the most powerfull Pentium 80-286 at the time..

The passion for programming continued through my highschool years at the Information Technology Institute, where Java was introduced as a new exciting language to discover. Shortly after my studies had to be put on hold due to a high amount of unmissable opportunities presented for my chefing career.

Years later, my research for becoming a web developer has led to finally enrolling for a six months fullstack programming bootcamp. Due to the fast pace of the course, many lessons were provided each week for the vast majority of the students. Assessments were often required to verify the progress of each student in order to provide assistance when required. Therefore a lot of material was just brushed the surface of the massive extent like JavaScript, for example, in the short time availabe to cover.

At the moment I have recently picked up my web dev learning opportunity being part of the Certificate IV of Information Technology in Web Developing. This will represent the changes that will make possible the transition from my career of Senior Chef to a more constructive and complex programming experience.

Principles of Information Technology

  • Data Security

    Ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data through various security measures.

  • Scalability

    Designing systems and applications to handle increased workloads and growing user demands without sacrificing performance.

  • User Experience

    Creating intuitive and user-friendly interfaces to enhance user satisfaction and facilitate smooth interactions with technology.

  • Interoperability

    Enabling different systems and software to communicate, exchange data, and use the information to create a cohesive digital environment.

  • Reliability

    Building systems and networks that consistently perform as expected, minimizing downtime and ensuring continuous operations.

  • Accessibility

    Making technology usable and accessible to individuals with disabilities, ensuring equal access and opportunity for all users.

  • Privacy

    Respecting and protecting individuals' rights to control their personal information and data collected by technology systems.

  • Compliance

    Adhering to legal and regulatory standards to ensure that technology operations and practices are in line with industry-specific guidelines.

  • Adaptability

    Designing systems and processes that can adapt to evolving technologies, business requirements, and dynamic market conditions.

  • Sustainability

    Promoting the use of technology in an environmentally responsible way, minimizing energy consumption and reducing electronic waste.
